What is synesthesia

Synesthesia is a neurological phenomenon in which stimulation of one sense automatically triggers an involuntary experience in another. A sound becomes a color. A word carries a taste. A number inhabits a precise point in space.

For those who experience it, the associations are immediate, consistent across a lifetime, and as real as any other perception.

Researchers estimate that somewhere between 2 and 4 percent of the population has some form of synesthesia, though because many people assume their experience is universal, the true number may be much higher. It manifests differently in everyone who has it: no two synesthetic landscapes are alike.

For me, I associate sounds with colors based on their timbre (ie. different instruments or vocal qualities).

Living with synesthesia has made me deeply fascinated with the way that our brains make associations and find patterns. I created this tool as a reflection of how music looks to me: a window into a perceptual world that is hard to describe in words alone.
